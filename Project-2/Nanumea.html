About Nanumea Island

Nanumea is one of the nine islands of Tuvalu, located in the Pacific Ocean. It is a small atoll consisting of five islets: Nanumea, Lakena, Motu Foliki, Lafogaki, and Te Motu Foliki. With a land area of approximately 3.87 square kilometers, it is among the largest atolls in Tuvalu. The island is characterized by its pristine white sandy beaches, coconut palms, and a central lagoon. The population of Nanumea is around 500 people, who primarily engage in subsistence agriculture and fishing. The island's culture is rich in traditional practices, and it plays a significant role in the heritage and identity of Tuvalu. Nanumea's remoteness and natural beauty make it an important part of Tuvalu's natural and cultural landscape.
