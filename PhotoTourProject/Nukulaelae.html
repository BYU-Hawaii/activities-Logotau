Nukulaelae is one of the nine atolls of Tuvalu, located in the Pacific Ocean. It is a small and remote atoll comprising about 15 islets with a total land area of approximately 1.82 square kilometers. The central lagoon, which spans around 25 square kilometers, is an essential resource for the island's inhabitants, providing fish and other marine resources. Nukulaelae has a population of around 300 people who mainly engage in subsistence agriculture and fishing. The island is known for its traditional way of life, with strong cultural practices and community bonds. The natural beauty of Nukulaelae, characterized by its coconut palms, sandy beaches, and clear waters, makes it a picturesque and tranquil part of Tuvalu's archipelago.
